Welcome to Ramakrishna Mission Ashrama

A place of spiritual peace, harmony, and enlightenment in Chandigarh

"Arise, awake, and stop not till the goal is reached."
— Swami Vivekananda

Sri Ramakrishna Temple, Chandigarh

Ramakrishna Mission Ashrama, Chandigarh is a branch of the Ramakrishna Math and Ramakrishna Mission, headquartered at Belur Math, Howrah, West Bengal. The Ashrama was established to propagate the universal message of Vedanta as exemplified in the lives of Sri Ramakrishna, Sri Sarada Devi, and Swami Vivekananda.


Located in Sector 15-A, Chandigarh, the Ashrama serves as a spiritual and cultural center for the Chandigarh Tricity region including Panchkula and Mohali. The serene environment of the Ashrama provides an ideal setting for spiritual practices, meditation, and self-development.


The Ashrama conducts various spiritual and philanthropic activities including daily worship, meditation sessions, religious discourses, celebration of holy occasions, educational programs, and social service activities. It serves as a beacon of Swami Vivekananda's message of "Service to Man is Service to God" (Shiva Jnane Jiva Seva).

Our Activities

Daily Worship

Morning and evening arati, meditation, and prayers conducted daily at the temple.

Spiritual Discourses

Regular classes on Bhagavad Gita, Upanishads, and teachings of Sri Ramakrishna.

Meditation Sessions

Guided meditation sessions for spiritual aspirants and beginners alike.

Value Education

Programs for youth and students focusing on character building and ethics.

Library Services

Well-stocked library with spiritual literature and publications.

Festival Celebrations

Celebration of Durga Puja, Kali Puja, and other holy occasions.

Social Service

Various philanthropic activities serving the underprivileged.

Youth Programs

Special programs and workshops inspired by Swami Vivekananda's ideals.
